### SECTION F: WHAT IS A 'CLOB STOP' AND CAN IT FAIL?

**What it is:**
"CLOB" = Central Limit Order Book. It is the decentralized engine matching buyers and sellers. A "CLOB Stop" is an automated instruction your bot sends to the API saying: *"Watch this asset. If the price drops to 0.60, immediately create a Limit Sell order to liquidate my position."*

**How it can fail (The Failure Modes):**
1.  **The 5-Token Floor (Handled):** Polymarket rejects orders smaller than 5 shares. Your code successfully anticipates this (`if size_est < 5.0: skip`).
2.  **Collateral Lock (Handled):** If you already have a Take-Profit sitting at 0.96, the CLOB considers your shares "locked". If the price tanks to 0.60 and the bot tries to sell, the API will throw an error: `Not enough balance`. Your code successfully catches this with `client.cancel_market_orders()`.
3.  **The Flash Crash (NOT Handled):** If a market goes from 0.85 to 0.40 instantly (e.g., breaking news drops), your bot triggers a sell at 0.60. But because the price is already 0.40, no one buys your 0.60 order. You get stuck holding the bag to zero. 
    *   *Proposed Fix:* When the bot triggers a stop-loss, it should submit a **Market Order (FOK)**, not a Limit Order. Take whatever liquidity is immediately available to stop the bleeding. *If wrong:* We suffer higher slippage on the exit, but we guarantee the stop-loss executes.
